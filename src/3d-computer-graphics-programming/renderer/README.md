## Formulae

### Rotation of a vector

Given that:

$$
cos(\alpha) = \dfrac{x}{r} \\
\therefore x = r \cdot cos(\alpha)
$$

And:

$$
sin(\alpha) = \dfrac{y}{r} \\
\therefore y = r \cdot sin(\alpha)
$$

then rotating a vector yields:

$$
x^ \prime = x \cdot cos(\beta) - y \cdot sin(\beta) \\
y^ \prime = y \cdot cos(\beta) + x \cdot sin(\beta)
$$
